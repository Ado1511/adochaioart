Guinness: Interactive Social Campaigns

Role: Art Direction & Concept Creation

The Challenge

Encourage consumers to engage with Guinness in a playful way, transforming a traditional beverage into a catalyst for viral, user-generated content (UGC) on social media.

Concept 01: #iPlayGuinness

We reimagined the iconic packaging by integrating tactile strings over the classic Guinness harp logo. This turned the can into a miniature instrument, prompting consumers to record themselves "playing" the harp and sharing their performances online.

Concept 02: #GuinnessART

Taking inspiration from latte art, we introduced special tools in selected traditional pubs. Patrons could draw directly into the famous dense stout foam, creating personalized art and sparking viral drawing challenges.

Clalit Smile: The Anatomy Transit

Role: Art Direction & Visual Concept

The Challenge

Visually communicate a complex medical warning—that neglecting oral health can lead to severe systemic issues like heart attacks and dementia—without using graphic or intimidating medical imagery.

The Concept

We created "The Train of Bad Surprises" for Israel's largest dental network. By reimagining the human body's internal systems as a metropolitan transit map, we framed the mouth as the central station.

Using minimalist silhouettes and recognizable subway line aesthetics, we illustrated how neglected mouth bacteria seamlessly "travel" to vital organs like the heart, brain, and liver, turning a medical lecture into an instant visual realization.

My approach to visual communication is highly tactical; I look at a brand's challenge the same way one might analyze a complex football formation—finding the hidden spaces and connecting the right elements to create a winning narrative.

For me, art direction isn't just about aesthetics; it's about building comprehensive ecosystems. Whether I'm conceptualizing a 360° rebranding campaign, crafting an interactive digital experience, or developing my own independent streetwear label, my goal is always to create work that disrupts the expected and leaves a lasting cultural footprint.

When I'm not designing, my creative process is fueled by a deep passion for football culture. Whether I'm diving into the history of the game, analyzing tactical evolution, or hunting down classic apparel, I bring this same appreciation for heritage and strategic storytelling into every creative brief I touch.
